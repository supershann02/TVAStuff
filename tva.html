What are Hex Codes?

Well the technical term is hexadecimal time.

Hexadecimal is a numbering system typically used for computer applications. Hexadecimal time codes are similar but are composed of eight digits instead of sixteen and the function of these codes is to display the date and time using a sequences of numbers and letters.

I'll use the time I am making this as an example. Note: I will be using Greenwich Time Zone (GMT), as it was the official international unit of time for almost a century and it is still used as universial unit of date and time.

6080DCC2 --> Thursday, April 22, 2021 2:17:38 AM (GMT)

Hexadecimal time codes are rather simple to comprehend, especially with a converter. There are many converters on the Internet. This is the one I use: https://www.epochconverter.com/hex It converts the code into both GMT and the time zone of wherever you are.
